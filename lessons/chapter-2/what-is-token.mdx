In everyday life, we encounter tokens in various forms: a token could be an arcade coin that you use to play games, or a ticket that gives you access to an event. In the digital realm of blockchains, a **token** is basically a piece of data (often a number in a database) that represents some value or entitlement. Because blockchains are decentralized ledgers, owning a token means your ownership is recorded across a network and can be verified by anyone.
